## 1. Definitions

**Access Person:** Any supervised person of the Firm who:
- Has access to nonpublic information regarding clients' purchase or sale of securities, or
- Is involved in making securities recommendations to clients, or
- Has access to such recommendations that are nonpublic

**Beneficial Ownership:** Having a direct or indirect pecuniary interest in securities, including:
- Securities held in one's own name
- Securities held jointly with others
- Securities held in accounts for immediate family members
- Securities in which one has the right to acquire beneficial ownership

**Reportable Security:** All securities except:
- Direct obligations of the U.S. Government
- Money market instruments
- Shares of open-end mutual funds (other than those advised by the Firm)
- Shares of unit investment trusts
- Securities held in accounts over which the Access Person has no direct or indirect influence or control

**Immediate Family:** Spouse, minor children, and adults living in the same household

## 2. Standards of Business Conduct

All Access Persons must:

### A. Fiduciary Duty
- Act with honesty, integrity, and in good faith
- Place clients' interests ahead of personal interests
- Avoid activities that create conflicts of interest with clients
- Conduct all personal securities transactions consistent with this Code

### B. Professional Standards
- Maintain competency and strive for continuous improvement
- Exercise diligence and thoroughness in client matters
- Maintain confidentiality of client information
- Provide full and fair disclosure to clients

### C. Prohibited Conduct
Access Persons shall NOT:
- Use their position for personal gain at client expense
- Front-run client trades
- Trade on material nonpublic information
- Recommend securities for client accounts when there is a conflicting personal interest
- Accept gifts or entertainment that could compromise professional judgment
- Engage in fraudulent, manipulative, or deceptive conduct

## 4. Personal Securities Transactions

### A. General Principles
- **Client Priority:** Clients' transactions take priority over personal transactions
- **No Front-Running:** Access Persons may not trade ahead of client orders to benefit from anticipated market impact
- **No Cherry-Picking:** Fair allocation of investment opportunities between clients and Access Persons

### B. Restricted Periods
Access Persons are prohibited from trading in a security:
- **7 days before** a client transaction in the same security
- **3 days after** a client transaction in the same security

**Exceptions:**
- Transactions in diversified mutual funds
- Automatic dividend reinvestment plans
- Transactions in employer retirement plans (401k, etc.)
- Liquidations required by court order or operation of law

### C. Pre-Clearance Requirement
All personal securities transactions in Reportable Securities must be pre-cleared by the Chief Compliance Officer BEFORE executing the trade.

**Pre-Clearance Process:**
1. Submit request via email to CCO at least 24 hours before trade
2. CCO reviews for conflicts and restricted periods
3. Approval valid for 3 business days
4. If market conditions change, re-clearance required

### D. Blackout Periods
The CCO may impose blackout periods during which Access Persons may not trade certain securities, such as:
- During distribution of research reports
- When making material changes to model portfolios
- During earnings seasons for concentrated positions

## 7. Insider Trading Prohibition

### A. Prohibition
It is ILLEGAL and PROHIBITED to:
- Trade on material nonpublic information
- Communicate ("tip") material nonpublic information to others who may trade
- Trade in anticipation of a client transaction that will move the market ("front-running")

### B. Definition of Material Nonpublic Information
**Material:** Information a reasonable investor would consider important in making an investment decision

**Examples:**
- Unannounced earnings
- Merger or acquisition plans
- New product launches
- Management changes
- Significant litigation

**Nonpublic:** Not available to the general investing public through legitimate channels

### C. Consequences
Violation of insider trading laws can result in:
- SEC civil enforcement action
- Criminal prosecution (up to 20 years imprisonment)
- Fines up to $5 million for individuals
- Treble damages (3x profits or losses avoided)
- Termination of employment
- Industry bar

### D. Reporting Suspected Violations
Any Access Person who suspects insider trading must immediately report to:
- Chief Compliance Officer
- OR SEC Whistleblower Office (if involving CCO)

**Whistleblower Protection:** The Firm prohibits retaliation against whistleblowers

## 8. Gifts and Entertainment

### A. General Policy
Access Persons may not accept or give gifts, entertainment, or other benefits that could:
- Create a conflict of interest
- Compromise professional judgment
- Create the appearance of impropriety

### B. Acceptable Gifts and Entertainment
**Allowed:**
- Modest meals in connection with business discussions (<$100 per person)
- Promotional items of nominal value (<$50)
- Tickets to sporting or cultural events (<$250 per person per year from single source)

**Requires CCO Pre-Approval:**
- Any gift or entertainment valued >$100
- Multiple gifts from the same source totaling >$250 per year

**PROHIBITED:**
- Cash or cash equivalents (gift cards)
- Gifts or entertainment from clients (except nominal items <$25)
- Gifts conditioned on specific business actions
- Entertainment without a legitimate business purpose

### C. Reporting
All gifts and entertainment >$100 must be reported to CCO within 10 days

### D. Political Officials Exception
**ZERO TOLERANCE** for gifts to government officials or candidates:
- No gifts or entertainment to SEC, FINRA, state regulators
- Comply with MSRB Rule G-37 (municipal advisers)
- Comply with Investment Advisers Act Pay-to-Play Rule

## 11. Confidentiality

### A. Client Information
Access Persons must maintain strict confidentiality of:
- Client identities (except as required by law)
- Client account information
- Client portfolio holdings
- Client investment objectives and strategies
- Client personal and financial information

### B. Permitted Disclosures
Client information may be disclosed only:
- With client's written consent
- As required by law or regulation
- To service providers under confidentiality agreements
- In response to legal process (subpoena, court order)

### C. Regulation S-P Compliance
The Firm complies with SEC Regulation S-P (Privacy of Consumer Financial Information):
- Annual privacy notice to clients
- Opt-out rights for certain disclosures
- Safeguards to protect client information

### D. Data Security
Access Persons must:
- Use strong passwords and enable MFA
- Encrypt sensitive client data
- Avoid discussing client information in public places
- Lock computers when away from desk
- Report data breaches immediately to CCO

## 12. Enforcement

### A. Violations
Violations of this Code may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Mandatory retraining
- Suspension of trading privileges
- Financial penalties (disgorgement of profits)
- Suspension from employment
- Termination of employment
- Referral to regulatory authorities

### B. Sanctions Considerations
CCO will consider:
- Nature and severity of violation
- Whether violation was intentional or negligent
- Whether violation harmed clients
- Prior disciplinary history
- Cooperation in investigation

### C. Appeals
Access Person may appeal sanction to Principal within 10 business days

### D. No Retaliation
The Firm prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Reports suspected violations in good faith
- Participates in investigations
- Refuses to engage in violations
