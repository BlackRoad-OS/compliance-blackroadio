## 4. Trading and Execution Supervision

### A. Best Execution

**Policy:**
The Firm has a duty to seek best execution for client trades, considering:
1. Price
2. Speed of execution
3. Likelihood of execution and settlement
4. Size of trade
5. Nature of the security
6. Commission costs

**Broker-Dealer Selection:**
- Maintain approved broker list
- Annual review of execution quality
- Consider research and services provided (soft dollars if applicable)
- Document broker selection rationale

**Monitoring:**
- CCO reviews trade confirmations quarterly
- Sample trades for price comparison vs. benchmark (VWAP, TWAP)
- Identify outliers and investigate
- Document review and findings

### B. Trade Allocation

**Fair Allocation Policy:**
1. **Pro Rata:** Default method based on target allocation %
2. **Partial Fills:** Allocate proportionally across all participating accounts
3. **Trade Errors:** Allocate to error account (not cherry-pick favorable trades)

**Prohibited Practices:**
- ‚ùå Cherry-picking (allocating profitable trades to favored accounts)
- ‚ùå Front-running (trading ahead of client orders)
- ‚ùå Late trading (accepting orders after market close)
- ‚ùå Market timing (in mutual funds where prohibited)

**Supervisor Review:**
- CCO reviews trade allocations monthly
- Verify pro rata methodology applied
- Investigate any deviations from standard allocation
- Document exceptions and rationale

### C. Trade Errors

**Error Identification:**
Errors include:
- Unauthorized trades
- Incorrect security purchased/sold
- Incorrect quantity
- Wrong account
- Price discrepancies

**Error Resolution:**
1. **Immediate:** Notify CCO upon discovery
2. **Investigation:** Determine cause and responsible party
3. **Correction:** Correct trade promptly (same day if possible)
4. **Client Impact:** If client harmed, make client whole
5. **Documentation:** Complete trade error report
6. **Prevention:** Identify systemic issues and remediate

**Trade Error Account:**
- Maintain separate error account at custodian
- Gains/losses from errors absorbed by Firm (not clients)
- Quarterly review of error account activity

## 10. AML/OFAC Supervision

### A. Customer Identification Program (CIP)

**CCO Responsibilities:**
1. Verify all new clients undergo CIP before account opening
2. Review identification documentation (copy of ID in client file)
3. Verify OFAC screening completed and documented
4. Approve high-risk clients (PEPs, foreign nationals, cash-intensive businesses)

**Supervision Procedures:**
- Weekly review of new account openings
- Verify CIP checklist completed
- Confirm ID documents collected and verified
- OFAC screening results documented

### B. Ongoing OFAC Screening

**Daily Screening:**
- Automated screening of client database against OFAC SDN list
- Alerts generated for potential matches
- CCO investigates all alerts within 24 hours

**Match Resolution:**
1. **False Positive:** Document reason (different DOB, address, etc.)
2. **True Match:** Immediate freeze of account + regulatory reporting (OFAC, FinCEN)
3. **Uncertain:** Escalate to legal counsel

**Recordkeeping:**
- Maintain log of all OFAC screening results
- Document match investigations
- Retain for 5 years

### C. Suspicious Activity Monitoring

**Red Flags:**
- Large cash deposits or withdrawals
- Structuring (deposits just under $10K)
- Unusual wire transfers (foreign, high-risk jurisdictions)
- Rapid movement of funds (in and out same day)
- Client evasive about source of funds
- Transactions inconsistent with client profile

**Investigation:**
1. CCO reviews flagged activity
2. Interview client if appropriate
3. Document investigation findings
4. Determine if SAR filing required

**SAR Filing (FinCEN Form 111):**
- File within 30 days of detection
- Report threshold: $5,000 (if known or suspected criminal activity)
- Maintain copy of SAR and supporting documentation for 5 years
- **DO NOT** notify client of SAR filing (tipping off prohibited)
